Nursing Pathways

NMBA Guidelines — Effective 2 September 2025
NP

Nurse Practitioner

Can prescribe AND administer Schedule 4 cosmetic injectables within scope.

  • In-person or video consult required before each prescription
  • Professional indemnity insurance mandatory
  • CPD must include cosmetic-specific activities
Note: Working solely in cosmetics is unlikely to satisfy NMBA advanced practice requirements for NP endorsement.
RN

Registered Nurse

Can administer under a valid prescription. Cannot prescribe independently.

  • Min. 1-year post-registration nursing experience required first
  • Must work under an authorised prescriber arrangement
  • Responsible for supervising ENs in the workplace
RNs with sole mental health, paediatric, or disability qualifications must first complete general registration.
EN

Enrolled Nurse

Can work in this field with specific restrictions — must always be under RN supervision.

  • Min. 1-year post-registration EN experience required
  • Plus 1-year in a related area (e.g. dermatology)
  • Cannot inject very high-risk areas (glabella, nose, forehead)
  • GP supervision does NOT satisfy NMBA requirement

Doctor Pathways

Medical Board of Australia Guidelines Apply

What You Can Do

  • Prescribe and administer all Schedule 4 cosmetic injectables
  • Work independently or own a clinic from day one
  • Provide prescriber oversight for nurses in your clinic
  • Full range of treatment areas subject to competency

Key Obligations

  • Must train and work within your scope of competency
  • Professional indemnity must specifically cover cosmetic procedures
  • TGA compliance for all products used and advertised
  • Informed consent and patient assessment are mandatory

Dentist Pathways

Dental Board of Australia Applies

Unique Strengths

  • Deep knowledge of facial anatomy, nerves, and vascular structures
  • Masseter/TMJ treatments within dental scope — very high demand
  • Natural cross-referral from existing patient relationships
  • Can expand lower-face aesthetics within existing dental practice

Regulatory Requirements

  • Current AHPRA registration with Dental Board required
  • Must be within your scope of practice and clinical competence
  • State/territory drugs and poisons legislation applies
  • Indemnity insurance must specifically cover cosmetic procedures

Training Considerations

What to look for in a training provider

Not all training is equal. These seven factors genuinely determine your readiness for practice — and your long-term success.

Live Model Training

Hands-on practice with real patients is the only way to build genuine clinical skill. Mannequin-only training does not translate to patient care.

Small Group Sizes

More trainees per trainer means less individual attention. Look for strict limits — 4–5 trainees per trainer is optimal for genuine skill development.

Actively Practising Trainers

Trainers should currently be working in the field. Real-world case experience is irreplaceable and cannot be replicated from textbooks alone.

AHPRA/TGA Compliance Taught

Training must cover the regulatory framework — prescriber requirements, advertising rules, and consent obligations are not optional knowledge.

Business and Career Guidance

Practical support on prescriber arrangements, job market navigation, and business setup moves you from training to practice significantly faster.

Honest Post-Training Expectations

Reputable providers are transparent that foundational training is a beginning. Be cautious of any provider who claims otherwise.

Affordable and Accessible

Quality training should not require significant debt. Compare value — class sizes, live models, post-training support — not just the price tag. Genuine access to quality education should be a right, not a premium.

Regulatory Framework

Understanding the landscape

The four key regulatory bodies and obligations every practitioner must know.

AHPRA

Australian Health Practitioner Regulation Agency

Regulates all registered practitioners. Compliance with your National Board's code of conduct and advertising guidelines is mandatory. Non-compliance can affect your registration.

NMBA

Nursing & Midwifery Board of Australia

Updated guidelines effective 2 September 2025 outline specific requirements for NPs, RNs, and ENs. All nurses must refer to Appendix A for profession-specific requirements.

TGA

Therapeutic Goods Administration

Regulates Botulinum toxin and dermal fillers as therapeutic goods. Administering or advertising unapproved products is unlawful. All advertising must comply with the TGA Advertising Code.

CPD

Continuing Professional Development

All registered practitioners must undertake CPD relevant to their scope. If your scope includes cosmetic procedures, CPD must include cosmetic-specific activities. Keep full records.
